What We Measure

Each side of the card (Front & Back) is scored across four categories on a 1–10 scale.

  • Surface (S): print quality, scratches, dimples, stains.
  • Centering (C): left–right & top–bottom balance; worst axis governs.
  • Edges (E): average of all 4 edges (each 1–10).
  • Corners (K): average of all 4 corners (each 1–10).

Front and Back are graded independently using the same rubric. Front is weighted heavier in the overall score.

How the Score is Calculated

  1. Front = (Sf + Cf + Ef + Kf)/4
  2. Back = (Sb + Cb + Eb + Kb)/4
  3. SlabMetrics Score (SM) = 0.80 × Front + 0.20 × Back

We keep internal category/side scores to 1 decimal during grading; the final SM is kept to 2 decimals (e.g., 9.72).

How a Grade Comes Together (Example)

Front: Sf=9.5, Cf=9, Ef=9, Kf=9.5 → Front = (9.5+9+9+9.5)/4 = 9.25

Back: Sb=9, Cb=9, Eb=9, Kb=9 → Back = (9+9+9+9)/4 = 9.00

SM = 0.80×9.25 + 0.20×9.00 = 9.20 → Badge 9

If SM were 9.26, it would map to Badge 9.5. If SM were 9.76, it maps to Badge 10.

Global Rules

  • Creases breaking front ink/paper hard-cap Surface ≤4 on that side.
  • Authentic factory defects (light roller/print dot): limit penalty to −0.5 to −1.0 if endemic to the issue.
  • Autographs on-card are not penalized; smears count under Surface.
  • Stains/Residue: grade as-is. Back is slightly more tolerant unless severe.
  • Transparency line (on card pages): “SlabMetrics suggested score equates to this badge grade.”

Tie-Breakers & Rounding

  • Internal category/side scores keep 1 decimal; SM displays at 2 decimals.
  • Badge mapping uses strict thresholds (no upward rounding), except explicit top rule: SM > 9.75 → 10.
  • Exact ties on SM: prefer higher Front Surface, then Front Corners, then Front Centering.

FAQ (Short)

Why is the Front weighted more than the Back?

The front drives collector eye appeal and market value more strongly. Our mix (80/20) reflects that, while still respecting the back.

Can a card with perfect centering but a light scratch get a 10?

No. A 10 requires near-perfect across all categories. Even faint surface flaws typically place a card in the 9.5–9 range.

Do you match PSA/SGC/BGS/CGC grades?

We publish a transparent, consistent rubric. Our SlabMetrics Score is a suggested grade and may not match third-party results.
